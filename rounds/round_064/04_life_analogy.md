A wall map of a country shows ~500 cities; the same country on a desk-size map shows ~50 — selected by population threshold. Töpfer's law tells the cartographer exactly how many to keep at each new scale, with a different exponent for towns vs roads vs rivers. Applied to LLM distillation, a teacher with 10,000 training examples and a student 1/10 the size would retain ~sqrt(10) × selected_count examples, with different formulas for reasoning vs lookup vs format examples.
