In five years, long-running agents will accumulate enough small failures during operation — minor parsing errors, retried tool calls, edge-case mishandlings — that maintaining a high-uptime production posture will need an in-place repair mechanism rather than relying on offline retraining. The interesting design choice will be whether repair is scheduled (periodic global tune-up) or stress-triggered (a small failure locally exposes pre-positioned reserves that crystallize into refined behavior only where needed). Stress-triggered repair scales naturally with deployment scale; scheduled repair does not. Agents will carry latent fallback behaviors and alternative-policy reserves dispersed throughout their internal state, ready to be exposed by failure events and to consolidate into permanent local refinements without disturbing the global behavior. The result is a self-densifying runtime where the failure surface drives its own repair.
