Picture a household thermostat that, instead of one signal (temperature), integrates four: room temperature, outdoor humidity, electricity rate of the hour, and occupancy. The HVAC vent opens to a continuous aperture based on the combined state — not all-on or all-off, but partial — and the aperture adjusts every minute as any of the inputs change. The integration is cheap, always-on, and gives a single scalar that translates straight to actuator position. Conflicts among signals (cheap power says open, low occupancy says close) get arbitrated by a small fixed rulebook. Wrong aperture either way is real money: too open wastes electricity, too closed makes the household uncomfortable.
