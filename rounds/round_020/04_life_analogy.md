Think of how a vintage shop owner periodically rotates and culls inventory on purpose. Not because anything is broken — the items still sell — but because slow accumulation of stale stock would eventually choke the floor. So once a week she takes one slow-moving section and aggressively turns it: marks down, gives away, sells in bulk. The store experiences a small "loss" event by design, on a schedule, in a chosen patch. Customers see fresh stock; the store stays profitable. Without this proactive small turn, the store would eventually become unshoppable and the rescue would require a catastrophic full reset.
