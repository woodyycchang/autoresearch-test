In five years, the discipline of "suppress all errors" for production agents will give way to "proactively trigger small mosaic errors to prevent catastrophic ones." Agents will deliberately fail in small, controlled, scheduled ways across a patchwork of contexts so that cumulative state buildup does not reach catastrophic-failure thresholds. The pattern is mosaic, low-intensity, deliberate, with specific outcome goals — distinct from chaos engineering's broad random faults and adversarial training's targeted attacks. The discipline of proactively burning off small accumulations to prevent large ones will be standard practice for long-running agents.
