In five years, agents will need a regulator between their continuous decision-making capacity and the discrete-event world they act on (one tool call at a time, one token at a time, one external API at a time). The interesting design choice is not "how fast can the agent emit decisions" but "how is the continuous reasoning capacity converted into a regular discrete tempo." A continuous reservoir of capability fed through a discrete-event regulator is a fundamentally different architecture from buffer-and-flush. The regulator gives the agent a steady tempo, prevents runaway, and provides a clean handshake point for synchronization with external systems. The right tempo depends on context, load, and external clock.
