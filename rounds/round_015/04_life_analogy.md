Think of how a household water tap converts continuous pressure in the supply main into discrete glasses of water at human pace. The water main is always under pressure; the tap is a regulator that meters discrete portions on demand, with feedback (the user sees the glass filling) and a defined release mechanism. The system separates the continuous reservoir from the discrete usage event by a regulator that can handle backpressure, throttle, and synchronize with downstream consumers. Without the regulator, opening the system either floods or starves the consumer.
