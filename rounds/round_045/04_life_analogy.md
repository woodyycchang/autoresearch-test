Mouse cortex visual critical period closes around postnatal week 5. During the window, eye-deprivation rewires cortex; after closure, the rewiring no longer happens. The closure correlates with maturation of GABAergic inhibition. The Form-D claim is that the closure has the same structural cause as deep-network plasticity loss: saturation of representation entropy, after which further updates require ever-larger gradient steps that the network's stability constraints disallow.
