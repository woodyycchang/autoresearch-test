A mouse kept in constant dim light free-runs with a ~24.2-hour internal cycle, gradually drifting out of synchronization with the actual day. Add a daily light pulse (zeitgeber) and the mouse phase-locks to 24.0 hours within days. Take the pulse away and it drifts free again. Apply this to an LLM agent's background-task scheduler: phase-lock to user-activity cycles when they exist; detect free-drift when the agent is operating in a mismatched environment.
