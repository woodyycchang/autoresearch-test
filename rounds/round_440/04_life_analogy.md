# Life Analogy — Sardinian cantu a tenore quintina

The **Sardinian cantu a tenore** (UNESCO ICH 2005):
- 4 male singers in close circle: bassu + contra + boche + mesu boche.
- Bassu+contra use larynx-constricted overtone-rich phonation amplifying formants.
- An EMERGENT 5th voice (quintina) appears acoustically when 4 voices combine — neither singer produces it; it emerges from combined overtones.

**TENORES-QUINTINA**: a spectral-allocation scheme where K specialist attention heads each focus on a fixed RoPE frequency band, and an emergent K+1-th "phantom" output channel is COMPUTED as a function of combined overtone-style products of the K-head outputs (e.g., Hadamard-product cross-terms between adjacent frequency bands). The phantom channel does not have its own head/weights — it is fully determined by overtone combinations. Differs from R416 XALAM-FREQ (5-band RoPE allocation) by introducing EMERGENT PHANTOM CHANNEL via combined-overtone product.
