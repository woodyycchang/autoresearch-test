A spy embedding a coded message in a normal-looking letter: every word that carries information is a constraint on what the letter can say. To remain undetectable, the message must occupy a small fraction of the signal. To survive paraphrasing (a paraphraser re-writes the letter while preserving meaning), the message must reside in semantic invariants. The intersection of "uses little signal" and "is preserved by all semantic-preserving rewrites" is information-theoretically small.
