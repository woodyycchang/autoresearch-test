Think of a city tour guide who runs two different walking tours — a Food History tour and an Architecture tour — that happen to pass through the same plaza. When the guide is leading the Food History group through the plaza, she points at a specific corner café and tells the story of an early 20th-century coffee importer; the plaza is functioning as the food-tour node. When she leads the Architecture group through the very same plaza, she points at a balcony detail across the square and tells a story about the architect's competition with a rival firm; same plaza, completely different downstream content. A new tourist who happens to know the plaza from the food tour cannot use that knowledge to predict what comes next on the architecture tour. The plaza is a shared node, but the path determines the local interpretation and the next stop.
