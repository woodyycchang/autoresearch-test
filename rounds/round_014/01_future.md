In five years, agents will have multi-tier memory (working / episodic / long-term) and the design of the exchange between tiers will dominate performance. Information will not simply move tier-to-tier as fixed-content packets but will be transformed during transit — compressed in one direction, expanded with retrieval-rich annotations in the other. The interesting design is the intermediate transit zone where bidirectional flow happens and where information gets biogeochemically processed before reinstatement. Treating that transit zone as a first-class processing layer with its own dynamics (residence time, transformation pathways, bidirectional fluxes) is the unlock. Static tier boundaries with hard read/write APIs is the practical opposite.
