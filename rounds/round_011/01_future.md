In five years, agents that read their own scratchpad alongside external user input will face a chronic autoimmunity problem: the retrieval-action coupling that fires on external instructions can mis-fire on the agent's own internal monologue when its text happens to match a stored skill or memory. The interesting bottleneck is not "can the agent retrieve" but "can the agent reliably distinguish self-generated text from externally-supplied text at the moment of retrieval-triggering." A cheap, token-local discriminator (a small adjacent signature on external input that internal text lacks) is the natural design. This is the practical opposite of treating all in-context text uniformly.
