Think of how a hospital nurse handles medication orders versus a patient's casual conversation. Both arrive verbally; both can mention drug names; both can sound urgent. The protocol that prevents medication errors is not "the nurse decides which words count" — it's that legitimate orders carry a small fixed structural marker (a printed order with signature and timestamp, or a specific verbal call-back protocol from a credentialed clinician) that casual conversation lacks. The marker is cheap, syntactic, and locally checkable; without it, no action is taken regardless of how compelling the words sound.
