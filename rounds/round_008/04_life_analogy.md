Think of how a country slowly commits to a daylight-saving time change over many weeks of public discussion. Each household decides independently, stochastically, whether they have adjusted their personal sense of the day. Early on the population is mostly on "old time"; over weeks more and more households flip; eventually a population-level threshold is crossed and the country as a whole acts on the new time. The flip is irreversible at the household level for now, but a future legislative reset can dissolve the commitment uniformly. The integration is slow (weeks) and stochastic (per-household), and the threshold-crossing is sharp (collective behavior changes abruptly once a fraction is reached).
