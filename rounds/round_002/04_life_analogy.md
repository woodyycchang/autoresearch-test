Think of a hoarder's attic that gets sorted only when the heating bill spikes. Items get tagged on the way in (this is a tool, this is a memento, this is a one-off receipt). When the bill goes high enough, the homeowner climbs up with bins and works through the attic by tag: receipts get scanned and trashed, tools get cleaned and moved into rotation downstairs, mementos get boxed and stored away from active use. The cleanup is not on a calendar — it is triggered by a real cost pressure — and it deliberately preserves usable items (tools) while recycling junk (receipts) and archiving things with sentimental load (mementos). Once the bill drops, the homeowner stops cleaning and goes back to normal use of the house.
