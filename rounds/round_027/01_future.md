By 2031, LLM systems will be required by law to provably forget specific training examples on demand (right-to-be-forgotten enforcement). Current "machine unlearning" methods reduce performance on the unlearned target but leave the underlying representation intact, recoverable by adversaries with small fine-tuning budgets. The deep question is whether *true* erasure — information-theoretic obliteration of the unlearned signal in weight space — is even achievable in a gradient-descent-trained network. If not, this is a fundamental architectural constraint with regulatory implications. If yes, the technique would constitute a major capability advance.
