By 2031, mechanistic interpretability of LLMs will have catalogued ~50-100 reusable transformer "circuits" — small subgraphs implementing primitive computations. The induction head (one of the first discovered) is now textbook. The next intellectual step is to use these circuits as biological hypotheses: if transformers and biological neural networks both face the problem of pattern continuation, the circuit motif may be conserved. Specifically, the induction-head two-attention-head bigram-completion algorithm may have a direct analog in animal associative-learning circuitry (e.g., Drosophila mushroom body Kenyon cell to mushroom-body-output-neuron). If so, mechanistic-interpretability discoveries from LLMs could predict neuroscience findings.
