In five years, agents deployed in high-stakes operational domains (manufacturing-line monitoring, clinical decision support, security operations) will need internal-state ontologies far finer-grained than today's "healthy / unhealthy / unknown" coarse labels. The interesting design choice is whether the agent maintains its own bespoke taxonomy of operational states — twenty to fifty discrete categories specific to its deployment domain, learned through deliberate exposure — versus using a generic confidence/uncertainty scalar. The taxonomy approach gives operators labels they can act on by-type; the scalar approach gives smooth tracking but loses categorical structure. High-stakes deployments will converge on bespoke fine-grained taxonomies, the way arctic peoples developed dozens of distinct words for ice states because the categorical difference was a life-or-death distinction.
