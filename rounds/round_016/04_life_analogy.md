Think of how a serious birdwatcher develops a vocabulary of dozens of distinct categorical labels — not "small bird" and "big bird" but warbler-vs-vireo, fall-plumage-vs-spring-plumage, immature-vs-adult, juvenile-call-vs-adult-call. Each category is a categorical distinction the birdwatcher learns to recognize from cues that a casual observer misses. The taxonomy is purpose-built: it carves the world into the discriminations that matter for the birder's questions (identification, breeding range, behavior). Without the taxonomy, the birder is stuck at "small brown bird"; with it, every observation slots into a label the birder can act on.
