A numismatist receiving 100 Roman silver denarii of the same emperor measures the die-axis on each — the angle between portrait-up on the obverse and figure-up on the reverse. The 100 measurements give a histogram tightly clustered around 6 o'clock (180°) with a few outliers. From the histogram he infers the striking technique used (hinged dies vs free dies) and the workshop quality. Apply the same dual-output rotational measure to LLM generations: forward generation paired with reverse-conditioned regeneration gives a die-axis histogram diagnostic.
