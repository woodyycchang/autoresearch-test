Think of how a chiropractor adjusts a single joint and the patient reports relief at a distant body part the chiropractor never touched. The mechanism is structural: the joint is connected via fascia and nerve pathways to the distant site, so a local intervention propagates along the pathway. The chiropractor keeps a map of "intervention site → downstream effect" pathways developed from training and case experience. Practical work doesn't require explaining the full pathway; it requires knowing which site is connected to which downstream effect.
