In five years, online behavior modulation of deployed LLM agents will rely on small, targeted interventions at specific positions in the residual stream / attention graph rather than full retraining. The interesting design is whether the intervention sites are local (a specific token position, a specific attention head) or pathway-based (an intervention at one site propagates along a defined pathway to a downstream capability). Pathway-based interventions allow you to address a capability symptom by intervening far from where it manifests. The cartography of "intervention point → downstream effect" pathways will become a deployment-time artifact alongside the model weights.
