In five years, long-running agents will face a pattern where a single small misrepresentation (an early factual error, a subtly biased prior, a misformatted memory entry) propagates by template-style contact to nearby representations, producing a cumulative drift that looks like a slow corruption rather than a single broken update. The interesting design is whether the propagation mechanism is structural — one misfolded representation templating others by contact — or merely statistical aggregation. The former gives a sharp, predictive theory of cascade timing; the latter only post-hoc explanations. Detecting and containing template-propagated misrepresentations before they reach critical density will be a runtime concern.
