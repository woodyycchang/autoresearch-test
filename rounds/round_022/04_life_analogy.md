Think of how a single misfiled receipt in an office's accounting binder can quietly corrupt the quarterly books. Not because the receipt is itself a large error, but because the next person who copies the binder forward copies the misfile too. Each subsequent copy inherits and propagates the error; by the time anyone notices, the books have a self-reinforcing pattern of mistakes that can't be untangled by editing one entry. The mechanism is template-style copying: each copy preserves the error, and the error reproduces in proportion to how many people consult the binder.
