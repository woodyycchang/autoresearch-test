In five years, online interpretability and steering of deployed LLMs will need cheap diagnostics that can be extracted at every inference step without doubling compute. The bottleneck is not the depth of internal representations — those are richly studied offline — but the bandwidth of what you can probe live: a single residual-stream layer, a small projection, a top-K logit fingerprint. The interesting design choice is whether to use that limited bandwidth as a continuous scalar (one health number) or as a structured qualitative taxonomy (a small fixed vocabulary of distinct "qualities" that the practitioner can recognize from the same probe). A taxonomy approach gives discrete labels that humans and downstream systems can act on quickly; a scalar approach gives smooth tracking but loses the categorical structure that operators actually use. Live agent operations will likely converge on a taxonomy-based diagnostic on a single cheap probe.
