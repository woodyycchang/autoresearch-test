Think of how a windowsill houseplant gets used to its specific orientation over months. If you rotate the pot suddenly, the plant takes a noticeably long time to re-orient — and the longer the plant has been in its current orientation, the longer the re-orientation takes. The plant has accumulated structural commitments (specific leaves turned a specific way, specific stems leaned a specific direction) that resist quick change. The relaxation time to a new orientation isn't constant; it grows with how long the plant has been in its old configuration.
