In five years, long-running deployed LLM agents will show aging effects: relaxation times to respond appropriately to novel inputs grow with deployment age, and the model gets harder to perturb out of its current operational state the longer it has been there. The interesting empirical observation will be whether this aging follows a power-law (consistent with glassy dynamics in disordered systems) or exponential (consistent with diffusion in a single basin). Power-law aging would mean the model's behavior is well-described by spin-glass-style metastable landscape with many shallow minima; corrections via fine-tune would have to navigate the landscape rather than just push gradient. Diagnosis of aging vs drift becomes critical.
