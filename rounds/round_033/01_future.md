By 2031, the connection between LLM-internal training dynamics and developmental neuroscience will be a major research direction. The grokking phenomenon (delayed generalization after training-loss convergence) has a specific empirical signature: weight-decay-driven transition from memorization to generalization at a sharp time point. This Form-D candidate proposes that the LLM phenomenon predicts a corresponding window in mammalian neurodevelopment where consolidation continues after surface-level mastery.
