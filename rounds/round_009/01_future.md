In five years, long-running agents will be characterized less by single-axis metrics ("good" or "bad", "helpful" or "harmful") and more by a small fixed constitutional balance across distinct operational tendencies: exploration vs exploitation, transformation vs preservation, openness vs consolidation. Each tendency is healthy in proportion; the agent's "constitution" is its baseline mix; pathology is drift from that mix, not absence of any one tendency. Diagnosis becomes a small constitutional profile rather than a scalar. Treatment becomes specific to the tendency that drifted, applied via opposite-quality counter-interventions. This is the practical opposite of single-axis safety scoring and the practical opposite of all-or-nothing capability gating.
