# R323 — life analogy

## Source: Pillar coral synchronous broadcast spawning
- All sexually mature coral colonies in a reef release gametes simultaneously on a specific lunar-temperature trigger.
- Triggered by darkness window between sunset and moonrise after full moon.
- Mass synchronization enables fertilization despite immobile parents.

## LLM analogy
**REEF-SPAWN**: multi-agent system where N agents work independently on a task until an EXTERNAL SHARED CLOCK signal fires, at which point all agents simultaneously broadcast their current candidate response to a shared aggregation barrier. Aggregator merges via voting/consensus. Synchronization is barrier-driven, not chained debate rounds.

## Differs from prior art (claim)
S2-MAD has independent first-round generation then group consensus. MAD with Adaptive Stability does barrier-stopping. CortexDebate uses sparse graph. REEF-SPAWN's externally-clocked synchronous barrier is functionally similar to barrier-based debate consensus.
