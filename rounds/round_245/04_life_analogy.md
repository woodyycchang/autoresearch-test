# R245 — life analogy

## Source domain: wabi-sabi
- Aesthetic principle of valuing IMPERFECT, IMPERMANENT, INCOMPLETE. A cracked tea cup, asymmetric pottery, weathered wood is preferred to flawless mass-produced equivalents.
- The "imperfections" are NOT noise — they are MEANINGFUL signal of process, time, hand. The crack reveals the hand-thrown nature of the pot; the patina reveals age.
- Cultivated: practitioners deliberately introduce or preserve small irregularities. Kintsugi gold-fills cracks to celebrate, not hide, the break.

## LLM analogy candidate
**Wabi-sabi response calibration**: deliberately train an LLM to EXPRESS calibrated uncertainty as INTENTIONAL aesthetic imperfection — leaving small explicit "cracks" of acknowledged ambiguity, partial knowledge, hedging clauses — rather than projecting smooth, confident, polished answers. Each response includes: (1) a "wabi confidence vector" — explicit per-claim confidence; (2) "sabi annotation" — visible patina of which claims rely on retrieval vs reasoning vs training-memory vs guess; (3) "kintsugi seams" — explicit citations of where the model's training cutoff or knowledge gap lies, gilded with phrasing that highlights rather than hides them. Trained via RLHF where polished-but-overconfident answers are penalized and visibly-uncertain-but-calibrated answers are rewarded. Distinct from standard calibration: aesthetic dimension (the imperfection is VALORIZED, not minimized).

## What differs from prior art (claim)
LLM Uncertainty Quantification (2506.07461, 2511.00280, 2505.23854) focuses on TECHNICAL calibration metrics. Linguistic verbal uncertainty work expresses uncertainty in language but as a technical tool, not as aesthetic principle. The wabi-sabi framing — that visible imperfection is the GOAL, not a tolerated cost — is conceptually distinct.
