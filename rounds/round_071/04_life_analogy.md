A summer day on a Greenland glacier: supraglacial lakes pulse meltwater into moulins; the moulins drop water 1 km vertically through englacial conduits; at the bed, the water gathers into a few large channels and runs to the terminus in 24 hours. Without moulins, the system reverts to slow distributed flow. The bimodal hydrology is the structural feature. A training data pipeline with the same structure has fast-lane and slow-lane partitions reorganized per epoch.
