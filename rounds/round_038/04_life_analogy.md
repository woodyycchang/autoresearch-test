A crowd at a concert: individuals at first clap in random phases. As applause intensifies, a synchronization phase transition occurs — the crowd suddenly claps in unison. Two physics mechanisms describe this: superradiance (when N independent emitters become phase-locked, the total emitted intensity scales as N² instead of N), and Landau symmetry breaking (the rotational symmetry over clapping phases is broken into a specific shared phase). The joint mechanism — coherent coupling AND broken-symmetry order parameter — captures the transition.
