By 2031, large multi-agent LLM populations will be analyzed as macroscopic many-body systems with phase transitions. The Form-A conjunction proposes that two source-domain mechanisms — Dicke superradiance (collective spontaneous emission from coherent atoms) and Landau symmetry breaking — *jointly* explain when an LLM-multi-agent population transitions from random uncoordinated outputs to coherent collective behavior. Neither alone is sufficient: superradiance gives the cooperative-coupling intuition; symmetry-breaking gives the order-parameter framing.
