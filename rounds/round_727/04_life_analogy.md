# Life analogy — undefinability gap

A person cannot reliably introspect on their own future emotional state in unfamiliar contexts; the prediction systematically diverges from outcome. The structural reason: the predicting-self uses the same machinery as the experiencing-self, so cannot fully model its own ground truth. LLM analog: a model asked to score its own output uses overlapping representations; the gap is the model-theoretic undefinability shadow.
