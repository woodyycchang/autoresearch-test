A monkey shown a new rule (e.g., 'choose the red one when the cue is high tone') will learn it within 5-10 trials. Primate PFC neurons encode the active rule in their persistent firing pattern, switching abruptly when the rule changes. LLMs do the same via in-context learning: shown new exemplars in the prompt, they generalize within the prompt with no parameter updates. The Form-D question: does the LLM mechanism (an induction-head plus a working-memory register) predict a specific signature of PFC neuronal activity during rapid rule binding?
