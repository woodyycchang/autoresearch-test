A locksmith asked to certify that a self-rekeying lock will never accept a particular forbidden key: she cannot in general inspect all possible future internal states of the lock and verify the property by induction. The locksmith's only options are (a) restrict the lock's mechanism so the state space is small enough to enumerate, or (b) accept that monitoring will only ever be retrospective. The choice between restricting the system or watching it post-hoc is the analog of choosing between bounded-architecture LLM agents (so safety becomes decidable) versus unconstrained self-modifying agents (so safety is Rice-undecidable).
