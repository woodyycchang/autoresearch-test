By 2031, LLM agents will be allowed to modify their own prompts, scaffolding, and weights to improve task performance, with the central regulatory question being whether their post-modification safety properties can be guaranteed. The deep theoretical question is whether *any* non-trivial safety property of a self-modifying LLM agent is decidable. If the answer is no — by reduction to Rice's theorem — then post-deployment safety monitoring is fundamentally limited, and regulatory frameworks must instead rely on architectural constraints. The exact analogue and the open question are the candidate.
