By 2031, retrieval-augmented generation will be the dominant LLM deployment pattern. Engineers will need quantitative scaling laws to budget retrieval depth k vs context window: how many retrieved chunks does it take before marginal relevance falls below some threshold? The current literature gives empirical relevance-vs-k curves for individual systems but no published universal scaling form. A predicted closed-form curve would let practitioners compute optimal k from corpus statistics alone. If the curve is Zipfian with a specific exponent across corpora, that would be a paradigm-shift result for retrieval architecture.
