## Scan focus
Catastrophe theory (Thom's classification, cusp catastrophe) as mechanism source for basin-stability analysis of LLM capability emergence. The cusp catastrophe V(x; a, b) = x⁴/4 + ax²/2 + bx parametrizes a 2D control space (a, b) and gives a fold curve where the number of equilibria changes from 1 to 3 to 1 — the canonical model of discontinuous phase transitions under continuous parameter change.

## Motivation
shared_math_structure: emergent abilities in LLMs show phase-transition-like discontinuities (Wei et al. 2022). The cusp catastrophe is the simplest mathematical model exhibiting this fold-bifurcation behavior. While the surface analogy is well-known, applying the cusp's hysteresis structure as a basin-stability monitor for capability-emergence training is less explored.
