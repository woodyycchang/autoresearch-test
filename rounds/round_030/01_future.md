By 2031, deploying 50-500 LLM agents on a shared task without central orchestration will be routine. The hard problem becomes *spatial differentiation*: how do agents in different parts of the population assume different roles without explicit assignment? Biological morphogenesis solves this via reaction-diffusion fields (Turing 1952): a chemical gradient breaks symmetry and locally instantiates different cell fates. A similar mechanism over a "policy-gradient field" could let LLM agent populations spontaneously differentiate. The conjunction of morphogenetic-field spatial differentiation AND a Turing-stable reaction-diffusion attractor is what produces robust pattern formation, neither alone.
