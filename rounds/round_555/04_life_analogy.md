# Life Analogy — Marshall Islands stick-chart wayfinding: pre-voyage memorized wave-map + 4-swell decomposition + island-disruption-cascade + sensory-pitch routing during transit

The **Marshall Islands stick chart** (wayfinding):
- 3 chart types: rebbelib (whole island chains, large map) + meddo (small region) + mattang (teaching tool).
- 4 named ocean swells: rilib (backbone, NE trade winds, year-round) + kaelib + bungdockerik + bundockeing.
- Stick chart shows how swells refract/reflect around islands; islands shown as shells at lashed junctions.
- Charts MEMORIZED before voyage; never consulted at sea — wayfinder lies prone in canoe to feel underlying swell pitch.
- Information cascades: deep-ocean primary swell → island disruption → reflected/refracted swell pattern → sensed by canoe → wayfinder decision.
