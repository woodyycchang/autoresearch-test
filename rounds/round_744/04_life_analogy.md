# Life analogy — spectral sequence depth control

A spectral sequence is a machine for organizing complex information accumulation across stages. In a study where you measure a quantity at multiple resolutions (zoom levels), the spectral sequence tracks how information at each level relates to the total: when adding finer resolution stops contributing new structure, you have convergence. LLM analog: each transformer layer adds finer resolution to the representation; the spectral-sequence convergence test indicates when adding more depth is wasteful.
