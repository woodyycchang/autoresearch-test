When an octopus settles on a coral reef, no central image is computed; instead each square millimeter of skin samples the local visual texture and locally fires its chromatophores to match. The overall camouflage is a tile-by-tile match. An LLM with per-position controllers doing the same — each looking only at its small neighborhood — would behave the same way: the global output emerges from local agreements, with no central arbiter.
