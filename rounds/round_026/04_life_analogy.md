Termite mound construction: thousands of workers build complex multi-chamber structures with no central blueprint. Two compounded mechanisms are at work. First, stigmergy: each worker reads the local state of the mound (humidity, CO2 gradient, pheromone trails on prior deposits) and chooses its next deposit location based on that state, never communicating with other workers directly. Second, a one-way ratchet on physical material: deposited mud hardens irreversibly within minutes, so the partial work cannot be undone by later actions — every increment locks in. The two together (sense-the-state-AND-irreversibly-commit) is what allows a colony of independent agents with no shared plan to converge on a coherent multi-chamber structure. Neither alone would work: pure stigmergy without a hardening ratchet would oscillate; pure ratchet without stigmergy would build randomly. The joint mechanism is what scales.
