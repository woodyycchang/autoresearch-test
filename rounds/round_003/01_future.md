By 2031, long-running agents will produce hundreds of factual claims and procedural commitments per task — "I have already verified X," "I have purchased Y on behalf of the user," "the database table Z exists." Downstream steps will reuse these claims as load-bearing premises. The bottleneck shifts from generating new claims to enforcing that earlier claims remain stable: if the agent contradicts a prior load-bearing claim mid-trajectory without an explicit recovery protocol, every downstream conclusion built on the prior claim is silently corrupted. The discipline of "you cannot retroactively repudiate a claim other steps relied on" needs to be enforced architecturally, not by hoping the model behaves. Reliance-aware consistency tracking — tagging which prior claims downstream steps actually used, then locking those claims under penalty of explicit retraction — will be a core piece of agent runtime. Evaluations will measure load-bearing-claim retraction rate as a first-class metric.
