By 2031 retrieval-augmented agents will need to handle skewed query distributions: a few hot topics consume 80% of queries while a long tail of rare queries fills the remaining 20%. A uniform-cost retrieval system serves the tail poorly and over-serves the hot topics. An asymmetric, throughput-shaped retrieval architecture — fast conduits for hot topics, slow matrix for the tail — would match the workload to the access mechanism. Self-pruning behavior (cold paths atrophy back to matrix) keeps the conduit footprint bounded.
