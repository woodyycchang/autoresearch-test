A limestone landscape over centuries develops a few large cave passages plus countless small fractures. Heavy rain flushes fast through the caves into a downstream spring within hours; a smaller portion seeps slowly through fractures and emerges weeks later. The same rainfall produces a fast spike and a slow tail at the spring. A cache for an LLM agent that splits hot queries (caves) from rare queries (matrix) reproduces this bimodal response, with the additional property that hot paths enlarge with reuse.
