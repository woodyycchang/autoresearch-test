In five years, agents will use the same span of context to drive two distinct downstream behaviors depending on a small structural cue — a deliberate "frameshift" that tells the model to re-read the same content under a different interpretive frame. The interesting design is whether the cue is positional, syntactic, or learned-token. The same prompt body produces helpful behavior in one frame and adversarial / safety-evaluation behavior in another, triggered by a small in-context marker rather than by recomputing the input. This is fundamentally different from generating multiple outputs from a single prompt: it is having two distinct policies read the same tokens differently.
