Think of how an air-traffic controller broadcasts a single short call — "United 232, descend and maintain flight level 240" — that ten different pilots hear and decode in fractions of a second. The format is rigid (callsign, instruction, altitude); the parameters are few; the encoding is optimized for many simultaneous receivers parsing cheaply rather than for arbitrary expressiveness. A free-form English sentence would be ambiguous and slow; the structured callout is compact and unambiguous. The format's value is exactly that it compresses the communicative content into a parameter pattern receivers know how to decode mechanically.
