In five years, agents will routinely manipulate small amounts of in-context state that need to function both as data and as policy. The interesting design is whether such state remains a passive data artifact that a separate "routing layer" reads and acts on, or whether the state itself directly modulates downstream policy by virtue of its structural form — the way a context entry's syntactic shape itself triggers different downstream behavior without a separate decision module. Storage-as-actuator is structurally different from storage-and-actuator pairs.
