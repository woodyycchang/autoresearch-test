By 2031, RAG systems will be deployed in privacy-critical settings (healthcare, legal, finance), and provable bounds on retrieval leakage will be required. The Form-B impossibility claim: no RAG system using natural-language prompts can guarantee non-leakage of retrieved documents under partial-prompt-attack, by an information-theoretic argument analogous to the Shannon zero-error capacity bound — a deterministic decoder cannot achieve zero leakage when the encoder-channel admits any non-trivial input-output correlation.
