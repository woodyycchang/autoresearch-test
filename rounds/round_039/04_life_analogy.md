A librarian asked to answer questions about a private collection without revealing any specific document: a sufficiently clever questioner can extract bits about the collection by phrasing queries to bias toward different documents and observing which questions the librarian declines. The fundamental limit is Shannon's: if the questioner has access to a channel, they can extract information at the channel's capacity per query. Zero-leakage is information-theoretically impossible unless the channel is trivially zero-capacity. This is the impossibility argument for RAG prompt leakage.
