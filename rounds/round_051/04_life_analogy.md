Sound in a long corridor with random wall textures: a clap can echo across the whole hall (extended), or get bounced repeatedly between two closely-spaced reflectors and decay locally (localized). Whether the echo travels or traps depends on how the disorder of the wall surface interferes with the wavefront. Long contexts in an LLM resemble the corridor; activations resemble the wavefront; whether a feature carries information across many tokens or pins to a small cluster is the same trapping question.
