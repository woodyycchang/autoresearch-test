# Life Analogy — Andamanese Onge/Jarawa oral transmission via ancestor-anchored survival chain + vertical-horizontal duplication + environmental-trigger retrieval

The **Andamanese Onge/Jarawa oral tradition**:
- ~96 Onge + ~250 Jarawa surviving populations; no written tradition.
- Knowledge transmitted purely orally: storytelling + songs + dances across generations.
- Each story chained to a named ancestor; survival-critical knowledge encoded in ancestor-narrative (e.g., "Onge story of huge shaking + high wall of water" preserved across generations, fired retrieval in 2004 tsunami).
- Linguistic transmission both vertical (parent → child) and horizontal (peer-to-peer) — redundant duplication.
- Retrieval is environment-triggered: sensory cue (ground shake) selects ancestor-tagged narrative.
