A juror asked to certify her own impartiality: any verdict she returns that includes "and I am impartial in this verdict" is logically suspect, because the self-certification is part of the verdict being certified. Mathematics formalizes this as Löb's theorem: a system cannot prove its own soundness without either being inconsistent or being too weak to express its own truth predicate. LLM agents asked for self-confidence may face an analogous formal obstruction.
