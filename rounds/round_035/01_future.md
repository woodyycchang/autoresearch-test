By 2031, LLM agents will routinely be asked to introspect — to report on their own confidence, identify their own errors, and certify their own outputs. The deep question is whether *consistent* self-modeling is achievable: an agent that says "I am reliable" and whose reliability prediction is itself reliable. Löb's theorem in mathematical logic gives a formal answer in the proof-theoretic setting: a formal system cannot prove its own correctness without becoming inconsistent. The Form-B candidate claims this generalizes to LLM agents.
