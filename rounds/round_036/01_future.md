By 2031, hallucination rates will need to be predictable as a function of model size for risk certification. Current empirical observations show that scaling reduces hallucination but with diminishing returns. A specific quantitative scaling law — hallucination_rate ∝ N^β with β = 0.4 ± 0.1 in parameter count N — would let practitioners compute deployment risk without empirical sweeps and would identify when an architectural change has broken the scaling. This is a Form-C quantitative-prediction candidate.
