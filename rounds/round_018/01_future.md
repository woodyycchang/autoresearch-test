In five years, attention-based models will exhibit measurable non-local effects: tokens that the model does not directly attend to (low attention weights, or even tokens outside the attention span) will nevertheless shift downstream generation in predictable ways via residual-stream propagation. The interesting question is whether these non-local influences are quantifiable as a "gauge potential" — a context-frame property that the agent picks up phase from without local interaction. Treating low-attention or out-of-span tokens as a gauge field gives a principled framework for understanding "context noise" that should not, on naive attention-budget grounds, affect behavior, yet does.
