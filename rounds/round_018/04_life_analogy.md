Think of how the layout of a quiet office still nudges everyone's behavior even when no one is actively reading the room. A pile of stacked boxes in a corner, a heater that subtly warms one side of the floor, a clock ticking just at the edge of hearing — none of these are objects you're consciously attending to, yet they shape where you sit, how long you stay, what tone you adopt. Remove them and the office "feels" different in ways you can't immediately localize. The environment exerts a non-local influence on behavior that no single attended-to feature explains.
