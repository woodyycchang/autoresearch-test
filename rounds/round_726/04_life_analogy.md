# Life analogy — Riemann curvature context gating

A hiker on rolling terrain shortens their stride and looks more carefully where the slope bends sharply (high curvature) and relaxes attention on long flat stretches. The bending signal is *intrinsic to the terrain*, not to how the hiker is feeling. LLM analog: a reasoning trajectory in hidden-state space passes through regions of varying geometric complexity; the local curvature should drive attention gating.
