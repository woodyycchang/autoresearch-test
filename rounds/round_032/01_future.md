By 2031, context window utilization (the fraction of in-context information that actually drives output) will be a primary architecture-design metric. Current observations show that effective context shrinks with sequence length even as nominal context grows — but the published curves are empirical with no closed-form. A specific quantitative prediction — that effective utilization decays as exp(-d/τ) with τ ≈ 0.7 × nominal-context — would let practitioners design sparsity and chunking policies analytically. This is a Form-C quantitative-prediction candidate.
