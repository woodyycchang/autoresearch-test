Reading a long book: an attentive reader retains detail about the last few pages, less about chapters back, and only gist for the opening. The retention as a function of distance is well-modeled as exponential with a time-constant proportional to total reading time. The same shape — exponential decay with a length-proportional time constant — appears in many memory-and-recall systems. The candidate predicts that LLM context-window utilization follows the same form quantitatively.
