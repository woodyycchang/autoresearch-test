Two physicists drop two grains of sand into the same air column from the same height — the grains land centimeters apart because tiny initial differences amplify through turbulent eddies. The rate of amplification per unit time is the Lyapunov exponent; the directions of amplification form the Lyapunov spectrum. For a group of LLM agents passing messages, two near-identical starting conditions diverge similarly; measuring the divergence rate diagnoses the conversation's dynamical regime.
