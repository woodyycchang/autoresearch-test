In five years, agents trained on continuous streams of data (online RL, continual fine-tuning, rolling distillation) will not acquire capabilities smoothly. The interesting empirical observation will be that capability emergence happens in discrete bands at characteristic training-step intervals, separated by periods of apparent stagnation — not as a smooth curve, not as a sudden one-shot phase transition. The mechanism will resemble reaction-diffusion: a latent capability variable accumulates from streaming data, hits a supersaturation threshold, "precipitates" into a stable capability, locally depletes the latent feedstock, then accumulates again at a new band-position. Predicting band locations and spacings — not just whether a capability will emerge — will be a productive evaluation axis. Continuous training will look like a Liesegang gel from the outside.
