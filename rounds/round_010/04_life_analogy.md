Think of how a saturated solution in a tall glass beaker, slowly seeded by a single crystal at the top dropping over time, doesn't precipitate everywhere uniformly. Crystals form first in a thin band near the seed, locally exhausting the dissolved supply, then a gap develops, then another band forms further down where the solution has had time to re-equilibrate. The bands are periodic, the spacing increases with depth, and the empty gaps between them are predictable from the diffusion-precipitation balance. A continuous input (the seeding) plus a depletion-and-diffusion dynamic produces a strikingly discrete and predictable banded pattern in space-and-time.
