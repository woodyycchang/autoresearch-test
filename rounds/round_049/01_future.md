By 2031, the LLM 'attention sink' phenomenon (early tokens absorb disproportionate attention regardless of semantic content) will be a well-understood low-level mechanism. Form D candidate: this LLM phenomenon predicts a corresponding cortical microcircuit observation — that primary cortex neurons should have a persistent baseline activity (a 'noise floor') functionally equivalent to attention sink, serving to stabilize representation dynamics. The reverse-direction prediction is specific: ablating the cortical 'sink' should produce same instability patterns as ablating LLM attention sinks.
