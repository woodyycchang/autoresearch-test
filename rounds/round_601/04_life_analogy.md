# Round 601 — Life-Analogy

**Welsh eisteddfod chair-ceremony** (Wales, 9th century onward; National Eisteddfod
1861-present): bardic competition with a 7-stage promotion ladder (englyn → cywydd →
awdl → strict-metre cynghanedd → preliminary judging → committee adjudication →
chair-bestowal) where each stage filters competitors by public adjudication. Master
bards (penceirddiaid) transmit canonical metre patterns to disciples (disgyblion) via
direct memorization + improvisation. The chair is bestowed only on a single annual
winner whose awdl satisfies cynghanedd consonant-pattern constraint AND public
adjudicator unanimity.
