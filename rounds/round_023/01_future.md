In five years, an agent's memory library will exhibit a coarsening dynamic: small, less-used memory items will progressively dissolve and redeposit on larger, more-used ones, producing a memory distribution that drifts toward a few large established items at the expense of many small specialized ones. The interesting design choice is whether to fight this dynamic (preserve specialized small items by replication) or work with it (accept coarsening as a useful consolidation). Predicting the coarsening rate and its dependence on retrieval frequency will be a productive line.
