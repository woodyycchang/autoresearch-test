A young soil starts as nearly uniform parent material. Over a century of rainfall, root activity, microbial decay, and clay-particle translocation, a vertically stratified profile emerges: dark organic-rich A at top, light-leached E below, clay-accumulated B further down, parent material C at the bottom. No designer set those boundaries; they emerge from the differential operation of four roles on the same substrate. A team of LLM agents operating similar role-differentiated transformations on a shared knowledge base would similarly stratify without explicit communication.
