A tall pencil-thin skyscraper has a 600-ton mass on a pendulum near the top tuned to the building's sway frequency; when wind pushes the building one way, the mass swings the other, cancelling the sway. Without the damper the building would amplify wind into structural oscillation; with it, the amplitude drains into the damper's frictional damping. An LLM in a failure oscillation is the same: each pass amplifies the failure pattern; a parallel module out-of-phase with the failure mode drains it.
