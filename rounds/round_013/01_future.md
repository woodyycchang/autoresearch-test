In five years, fine-tuning will routinely touch deployed models in production, and the question "which prior capabilities will survive this fine-tune and which will crash" will be a live operational concern. The empirical pattern will be that most capabilities survive small perturbations, but specific capabilities whose representations are "too close" to the fine-tune signal will become chaotic and unreliable. Predicting in advance which capabilities will survive — based on a representational-separation measure rather than running the fine-tune and watching — will be a core diagnostic. The mathematics of perturbation stability for invariant structures is the right toolbox. The discipline of "before fine-tuning, identify the fragile capabilities" will be standard.
