A buyer purchases a painting from a gallery, paying market price after standard inquiries. Later it transpires the painting was stolen decades earlier from a now-deceased owner. The buyer keeps the painting if they were a bona fide purchaser without notice — the duty of inquiry was met, the title is cleansed by the doctrine. This asymmetric chain-breaking — clean state can emerge from prior-tainted state via inquiry — has no obvious equivalent in current LLM runtime sanitization.
