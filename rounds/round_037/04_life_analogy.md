A child's visual cortex develops in stages: at birth, only thalamic LGN-to-V1 connectivity is functional; orientation-selective columns crystallize over the first few weeks; binocular disparity columns require both eyes to be open and form months later. The order is not random — earlier circuits feed later ones. The Form-D question: does the order in which transformer circuits form during pretraining predict this developmental sequence? If yes, LLM training trajectories become a predictive instrument for developmental neuroscience.
